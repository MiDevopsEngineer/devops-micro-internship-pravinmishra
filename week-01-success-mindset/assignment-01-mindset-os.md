# Assignment 1. What is something you believe to be true that most people around you would disagree with?

### Rules

* No "safe" answers.
* Must be your real belief (not copied from internet).
* Minimum 50 words.

**Hint:** What do you believe about career, money, learning, discipline, relationships, health, success, life, tech industry, etc. that most people don't agree with?

## Answer

One belief I strongly hold is that formal education is no longer the primary path to building a successful career. While a university degree can be valuable, I believe practical skills, continuous learning, and the ability to solve real-world problems matter far more in today's economy. Many people around me still believe that getting a degree automatically leads to financial security, but I have seen skilled individuals build successful careers through self-learning, online courses, certifications, and hands-on experience. I believe those who consistently invest in developing in-demand skills and adapting to change will have greater long-term opportunities than those who rely solely on academic qualifications.

## Truth #1

### Truth

Consistent effort applied over time produces better results than short bursts of intense motivation.

### Evidence from my life

When I first started working remotely, I focused on improving my skills every day instead of waiting until I felt motivated. I consistently learned new tools, completed certifications, and applied for opportunities. Over time, this approach helped me transition into project management, property management, and later DevOps and cloud security, leading to better career opportunities than I would have achieved through occasional effort.

### Truth

### Truth

# Assignment 4. Have you ever cut corners (unethical / dishonest / shortcut behavior — not necessarily illegal)? If yes, how did it make you feel?

### Important

You don't need to write the full story.

Focus on the feeling:

* guilt
* fear
* shame
* stress
* regret
* numbness
* etc.

This is about self-awareness, not judgment.

### Answer Format

**Yes**

If Yes:

**What emotion did you feel?** (minimum 50–100 words)

## Answer

I felt a combination of guilt, stress, and regret. Even when taking a shortcut seemed harmless at the time, it created a sense of discomfort because I knew it did not fully align with my values. Instead of feeling satisfied, I became concerned about whether my actions would affect my credibility or the trust others placed in me. The experience taught me that short-term convenience is rarely worth the long-term cost to my integrity and peace of mind. Since then, I have made a conscious effort to be more transparent, take responsibility for my work, and choose the ethical path even when it requires more time or effort. Maintaining trust and a good reputation has become far more valuable to me than achieving quick results.

# Reflection – Week 1

### Biggest insight I got about myself this week

I realized that my progress depends more on building consistent systems than on motivation. Whenever I follow a structured routine and focus on one priority at a time, I make meaningful progress. Long-term success comes from repeating the right habits consistently rather than relying on short periods of intense effort.

### My biggest weakness/loop I noticed

I sometimes spend too much time planning, researching, or trying to perfect my work before taking action. This delays execution and reduces the amount of practical experience I gain. I perform better when I start quickly, learn through doing, and improve as I go.

### One system I will implement from this week (exact habit + time)

Every weekday from 6:00 AM to 7:30 AM, I will complete one uninterrupted deep work session dedicated to learning, building, or documenting a DevOps or Cloud Security project. During this time, my phone will remain on Do Not Disturb, and I will focus on a single task until the session is complete.
